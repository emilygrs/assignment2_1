Vaccination Rates Among New York State Hospital Staff Across Regions of New York State

How New York Regions and Counties Differ in Terms of Hospital Staff Vaccine Rates for Tuesday Nov. 16

By: Emily Sauchelli, Nov. 24, 2021

The data set I chose to illustrate is from the New York State Department of Health, specifically the Office of Quality and Patient Safety within the Office of Primary Care and Health Systems Management.

I decided to compare all of the regions within New York State, specifically the amount of hospital staff members who are particially vaccinated v. fully vaccinated as of November 16, 2021. The data is very large, so I decided to break it down into reasonable charts, that display the names of each hospital of a particular region of New York State, along with the county that each hospital is located in. Each chart displays percentages that illustrate the vaccine status of hospital workers, for a particular day, Tuesday November 16, 2021.

On August 16, 2021, then Gov. Andrew Cuomo announced a mandate, by the New York State Department of Health, that all healthcare workers be vaccinated. In the vaccine mandate, he cited that all healthcare workers must have their first vaccine dose by Monday September 27, 2021. As defined in his announcement, healthcare workers include the following; staff at hospitals and long-term care facilities (LTCF), including nursing homes, adult care, and other congregate care settings.

After Andrew Cuomo resigned, Lieutenant Gov. Kathy Hochul took over in late-August 2021. "Vaccine requirements work in getting people to do the right thing, and all professionals in health settings must take every basic precaution against COVID-19, including the vaccine, so they do not spread the virus to the people coming in for treatment," Governor Hochul said during a press conference on Oct. 5. "We have an obligation to extend this assurance to those who need to receive mental health services and special care, which is why we are making the vaccine mandatory for all staff who work in these facilities that fall under State jurisdiction."

In using a pivot table to compare the data, I found that the Capital Region of New York State has the lowest average percent of hospital staff that are partially vaccinated at 0.8 percent. Mohawk Valley has the highest average percent of hospital staff that are partially vaccinated at 3.5 percent.

In using a pivot table to compare the data, I found that the Mid-Hudson region of New York State has the higest average percent of those hospital staff that are fully-vaccinated at 98 percent. Mohawk Valley, The Finger Lakes and Western New York all tied at 93 percent.
